# The Impact of Capitalism on Millennial and Generation Z Cohorts

## Introduction

Capitalism, as an economic system, has been the bedrock of modern economies, characterized by private ownership of the means of production, profit-driven enterprise, and market-based allocation of resources. However, its impact on society is multifaceted and has been subject to scrutiny, particularly in the context of its effects on younger generations, namely Millennials (born between 1981 and 1996) and Generation Z (born between 1997 and 2012). This report delves into the relationship between capitalism and these generational cohorts, examining how economic structures have shaped their experiences, values, and outlooks.

## Generational Discontent with Capitalism

Recent surveys and studies have highlighted a growing discontent among Millennials and Generation Z with the current capitalist system. A Business Insider survey found that 28% of Gen Z respondents expressed a preference for socialism over capitalism, a sentiment nearly on par with the 29% who favored capitalism (Business Insider, 2023). This ambivalence towards capitalism is further evidenced by the fact that a significant portion of Gen Z does not have a clear preference for either economic system.

## Social Issues and Capitalism

Millennials and Gen Z are increasingly concerned with social issues, which they believe are exacerbated by the capitalist system. Six in 10 Gen Zs and 56% of Millennials perceive systemic racism as widespread, and more than half believe that society may be on the cusp of meaningful change regarding racial justice (Forbes, 2021). This generation's focus on social change is also reflected in their consumer behavior and career choices, with many making decisions based on their personal values and the social impact of businesses (Forbes, 2021).

## Economic Challenges and Perspectives

The economic challenges faced by younger generations have shaped their perspectives on capitalism. High costs of housing, healthcare, and education are seen as failures of the current system (Business Insider, 2023). Over 50% of Gen Zers report extreme worry about not having enough money, and many have engaged in multiple jobs or side hustles to make ends meet (Forbes, 2023). These financial pressures have led to a reevaluation of capitalism's efficacy in providing stability and prosperity.

## The Call for a Reformed Capitalism

There is a call among Millennials and Gen Z for a reformed version of capitalism that is more socially and environmentally conscious. This new vision for capitalism is driven by a desire for greater stability, prosperity, and solutions to environmental problems (The Telegraph, 2023). The potential for intergenerational collaboration, particularly with Baby Boomers, is seen as a way to blend the idealism of younger generations with the experience and capital of older ones, fostering what some call "socially energized capitalism" (The Telegraph, 2023).

## Financial Attitudes and Behaviors

The financial attitudes and behaviors of Millennials and Gen Z differ significantly from previous generations. Many are seeking additional income due to the challenging economic environment and inflation (Bank of America, 2022). There is also a notable interest in alternative economic models, with 67% of young Britons expressing a preference for living under a socialist system (Dazed Digital, n.d.). This reflects a broader disillusionment with the capitalist model and a search for fairer alternatives.

## The Future of Capitalism and Generational Impact

As the largest intergenerational transfer of wealth is set to occur, the way Millennials and Gen Z handle this inheritance will shape the future of capitalism (The Telegraph, 2023). Their preferences for social and environmental activism may lead to a major shift towards a more socially responsible economic system. However, there is also evidence that support for capitalism remains strong among younger generations, with many valuing key characteristics of the system, such as property ownership and voluntary exchange (Forbes, 2023).

## Conclusion

The relationship between capitalism and the Millennial and Generation Z cohorts is complex and evolving. While there is a notable critique of the current capitalist system among these generations, particularly concerning its social and environmental impacts, there is also a recognition of its potential benefits. The desire for a reformed capitalism that aligns with their values of fairness, justice, and sustainability is clear. As these generations continue to gain influence, their preferences and actions will undoubtedly shape the trajectory of the economic system. The challenge for capitalism is to adapt and reform in ways that address the concerns of these younger cohorts while retaining the positive aspects that have made it a successful economic model.
